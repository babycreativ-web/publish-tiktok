A man stands on the edge of a skyscraper rooftop, ready to jump.
The city below is a blur of lights, a symphony of life.
He feels invisible, a ghost among the living.
A single tear escapes, carrying a lifetime of pain.
In his mind, a little girl's laughter echoes, a memory of happier times.
A gust of wind whispers, "You are not alone."
He sees a figure approach, a stranger with kind eyes.
"I've been where you are," the stranger says, offering a hand.
In that moment, hope sparks.
The man shares his story, each word a weight lifted.
The stranger listens, understanding reflected in his gaze.
As the sun rises, a new day begins, and the man finds the strength to step back from the edge.
But as he turns, he realizes the stranger is gone, leaving only a note: "I was you, years ago. Pay it forward."
Stunned, he understands the power of connection, ready to embrace life again.
